The Caribbean Flamingo

Flamingos get their pink color from the food that they eat, such as algae and saltwater shrimp. Their bodies digest the color hues from theses foods and this turns their feathers pink.

  • Scientific Name: Phoenicopterus ruber
  • Average Length: 3 - 3.4 ft.
  • Average Lifespan: 20 to 30 years
  • Habitat: Shallow salt/brackish waters

It is estimated that there are more than 200,000 wild, Caribbean Flamingos. The largest populations of flamingos are in Cuba, Aruba, The Bahamas, Galápagos Islands and Mexico. Overall, there are six flamingo species: Lesser, Caribbean, Chilean, Greater, Andean and Puna.

Source: Smithsonian’s National Zoo & Conservation Biology Institute
